VEX Robotics Competitions

Shaping the Engineers of Tomorrow

History & Impact

Founded by Innovation First International, VEX Robotics offers accessible, scalable robotics solutions for education. The program spans elementary to university levels, providing platforms tailored to various age groups and skill levels. In 2018, it was recognized by Guinness World Records as the world's largest robotics competition.

VEX Robotics competitions challenge students to design, build, and program robots for specific tasks within game-based engineering challenges. Divided into leagues like VEX IQ and VEX V5, these competitions promote critical thinking and innovation. Tournaments occur year-round, culminating in the VEX Robotics World Championship.

"The VEX Robotics Competition... continues to inspire, motivate and prepare kids for a future in advanced STEM education and related professions." — Jason Morrella, President of the REC Foundation

Did You Know?

VEX Robotics has a partnership with NASA and the REC Foundation to incorporate STEM concepts into classroom learning.

Most Influential VEX Competitions

Year Competition Name Importance
2015-2016 Nothing But Net Significantly advanced teams' understanding of projectile physics and control algorithms.
2019-2020 Tower Takeover Encouraged teams to develop versatile robots capable of both stacking and defensive maneuvers.
2021-2022 Tipping Point Showed the importance of center-of-gravity considerations in robot design.
2022-2023 Spin Up Demonstrated the importance of the integration of mechanical design with sensor-based programming.

Past Champions

The VEX Robotics World Championship has seen many champions. A notable winner is the team from Shanghai Jiao Tong University, which has won multiple championships.

This Year's Game: Push Back

In the 2025–2026 VEX V5 Robotics Competition game, "Push Back," alliances of two teams each compete on a 12’ x 12’ field. The match consists of a 15-second Autonomous Period followed by a 1 minute and 45-second Driver Controlled Period. Teams aim to outscore opponents by placing Blocks into Goals and controlling zones.

A video reveal of the game typically demonstrates the rules, scoring, and field elements to help teams strategize for the new season. This visual guide is crucial for understanding the engineering challenges ahead.

Game-Changing Strategies

A winning strategy often involves a robot that can quickly score in the first few seconds and then play defense to prevent the opposing alliance from scoring.
