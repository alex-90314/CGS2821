This Years Game

In the 2025–2026 VEX V5 Robotics Competition game, "Push Back," alliances of two teams each compete on a 12’ x 12’ field. The match consists of a 15-second Autonomous Period followed by a 1 minute and 45-second Driver Controlled Period. Teams aim to outscore opponents by placing Blocks into Goals, controlling zones within those Goals, and parking robots in designated zones at the match's end. The field features 88 Blocks, four Goals (two Long and two Center), and two Park Zones. Each Block scored in a Goal earns three points, with additional points awarded for controlling zones and parking. The alliance with the highest Autonomous Period score receives a 10-point bonus.

"Push Back" challenges teams to develop robots capable of efficient Block manipulation, precise movement, and strategic planning. The game's design encourages innovation in robot mechanisms and programming to maximize scoring opportunities. By emphasizing both autonomous and driver-controlled skills, "Push Back" fosters a comprehensive understanding of robotics principles, preparing students for real-world STEM applications.
